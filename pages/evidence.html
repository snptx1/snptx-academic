Evidence Boundary and Status Discipline

Claims are partitioned into implemented, partial, and roadmap states to avoid conflating architectural intent with validated operation. This section exists to make the document’s evidence posture explicit and to keep research infrastructure claims separate from stronger deployment or clinical interpretations.

Evidence summary

How the boundary is enforced

  • Operational features are described separately from partially integrated or still-planned capabilities.
  • Boundary statements are attached to specific capabilities instead of relying on broad implied maturity.
  • Clinical and commercial interpretations are explicitly excluded from the current evidence scope.
Implemented

Operational in the current build

Snakemake orchestration, MLflow experiment tracking, extension-runner execution model, 8 modality-family benchmark coverage, and autonomous campaign execution are operational.

Partial

Present but not universal

DVC is configured but not fully integrated into the primary pipeline. Feedback is operational in autonomous experimentation, not yet a universal framework contract.

Roadmap

Planned extensions of the current build

Cross-institution reproducibility pilots, full run-level commit linkage in tracking metadata, broader active-learning integration, and advanced deployment interfaces remain planned.

Status logic

Why the partition matters

The document is stronger when operational capabilities are not rhetorically extended into system-wide guarantees. This section keeps that separation visible.

Interpretation control

What the page is not claiming

Clinical utility, commercial deployment readiness, and universal reproducibility guarantees are not implied by the current benchmark and architecture evidence.

Section role

How Evidence supports the page

This section acts as the page’s internal scope control, limiting overstatement and making maturity boundaries explicit before later sections discuss intelligence, autonomy, or methodology.
